Strength Equipment

The strength equipment uses resistance forces such as gravity, body weight, and tension to build stamina & strength. Some of the strength equipment is as follows:-

FLEXIBILITY EXERCISE

Flexibility is important because it preserves your range of motion and reduces the risk of straining a tendon or pulling a muscle.

FREE WEIGHTS

When you think of exercises to build muscle, strength training is probably the first thing that comes to mind. For your legs, try the Smith Machine, leg presses, and squats.

LEG PRESS MACHIN

It comes in different shapes and sizes to provide a workout regime for lower body muscles with minimum impact on joints. This machine provides a good workout for every area of the lower body including thighs, hamstrings, glutes & calves.

ROWING MACHINES

It provides a kind of full-body workout focusing on the back, arms & legs at the same time. You can do basic level cardio with a rowing machine.

Cardio Equipment

You can do aerobic exercises using cardiovascular equipment. With these exercises, you can burn your calories while the increased heartbeat rate provides strength to heart muscles. Some cardio equipment, including different types of gym machines, are:

RESISTANCE BANDS

These are inexpensive, portable rubber bands or tubes that can be used for full-body workouts. Try different positions by placing hands or feet together and far apart on the band or tube to find out how you can exercise best with varying resistance levels.

BARBELL SET

If you are aiming for heavy strength training and not just weight loss, a barbell set is your go-to equipment. It also comes in handy for the senior age group & for beginners who are interested in increasing bone density. Other options in the barbell set are Olympic bar (20kg bar for men & 15kg for women), bench press rack and squat rack, etc.

TREADMILLS

The treadmill allows you to do walking, jogging, or running indoors at your own pace. Treadmill exercises primarily target your leg muscles and upper body if you jog or run. A treadmill is a right type of exercise equipment for beginners as they can start doing a moderate cardio workout.

ELLIPTICALS

This equipment is best for people who have joint disorders (e.g., osteoarthritis). It is a gym machine providing circular up and circular down motion that targets the lower body and arms while protecting your back, knees, and ankles from any impact.
